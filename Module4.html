Module 4

While the consequences or purposes of problem behavior in persons with dementia might seem endless, behavior scientists have narrowed these to just four: (1) Reduce stimulation; (2) increase stimulation; (3) escape from unpleasant situations; and (4) attention from others. Bear in mind that a specific behavior might serve more than one purpose; this is especially the case with persons in the later stages of dementia, as their repertoires become extremely limited and they try to make the best with what abilities they have left.

When determining whether the purpose of behavior is to reduce stimulation; increase stimulation; escape from an unpleasant situation; or get attention from others; the following questions will serve as a guide.

  1. Reducing stimulation
    • (A) Does the behavior occur when there is lots of activity in the person’s environment (e.g, loud TV or music, people talking)?
    • (B) Does the behavior stop when the patient is moved to a quiet environment?
    • *If you answer “yes” to both of these questions, chances are the purpose of the behavioral challenge is to reduce stimulation.
